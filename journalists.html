Fair Pay for Freelance Journalists

Join our newsletter for updates on IndyHub's work and to learn how you can help more.

Current Situation

A majority of journalists freelance in the US, and yet face significant issues, ranging from low pay, poor contract terms, unsafe working conditions, and poor payment periods. These issues have become especially acute given the huge layoffs in US media outlets. Freelancers are the backbone our media - essential to maintaining quality information to citizens - and yet many can not earn a living from it, work without essential safety equipment, and have to wait over a year for pay. The IndyHub is dedicated to campaigning to improve the working conditions for freelance journalists. Currently, there is a lack of transparency on freelancer conditions such as journalists not being allowed to speak publicly about their rates.

Our Call to Action

Change needs to happen. Our current campaign will focus on: Getting transparent information on freelance journalists' pay by publication, gender, and type of media. We will use Freedom of Information requests, public information, and most powerfully, data submitted by journalists themselves. We will use the information to create campaign priorities including specific payment rate, terms, and gender equality. We will also share data openly with freelancers to improve their negotiating position with outlets. We will continuously publicize information and work with fellow advocacy groups, lawmakers and media outlets to create fair conditions for freelance journalists. Using the Norweign example, we aim to create a comprehensive agreement with media outlets on minimum standards.

Contact us at hello@theindyhub.com. We'd love to hear any feedback, campaigning priorities, or information on freelancing conditions.
