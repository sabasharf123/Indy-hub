Our Demands

Living Wage

Employers must pay workers a livable hourly rate after expenses. Furthermore rates should be fixed for an agreed time period when signing up, giving personal investment needed.

Transparency & Representation

State wages and fees clearly and openly report harassment and abuse in the workplace while being clear why workers have been terminated and allow appeals. Hence there need to be expedient and clear responses to questions and concerns. At least one worker, chosen by workers, to have a voice at executive and board meetings.

Health & Safety

Ensure that all working conditions are safe. This includes mental health, fair break policies and protections from abuse from employers and clients.

Our Call to Action

Coordination

We’ll organize protests, demonstrations, and non-violent direct action, in order to highlight the worker violation that is happening

Boycotts

There is competition for all gig services. We will use this to coordinate worker, and user boycotts - forcing companies to address the issues.

Information

Companies are running a disinformation campaign to ensure they can continue worker violation. We will collect, distribute and report factual information on issues, occurrences and seriousness of issues at workplaces, through news outlets and our networks.

Our Manifesto

We live in a world where politics has become business and business has become politics. In the middle, independent workers rights have been violated and neglected with little care or protection. Facebook moderators face serious mental health issues, Uber drivers don’t receive any money from price increases, and companies even refuse to pass on tips earned by workers. Despite every effort, we, the workers, still have power. There is a better way of doing business and we can create it. Now is the time for change, now is the time for us to stand up for each other and for fair, equal work. The future will not solely be shaped by large corporations, but also by workers to create a more equal society.
