Once you’ve found your dream co-worker, you need to decide how to contract them, and set up payroll or invoice management. The first big choice is contractor or employee? This is an easier than it sounds as the IRS has clear guidelines on how people should be categorised.

If they are a contractor or employee - you still need to put a contract in place in order to protect both parties. Key terms in a contract are:

  • Confidentially – stating that they won’t share information received with other companies or individuals
  • IP – stating that all content created belongs to the company, not the individual creating it
  • Non-compete – that the individual won’t immediately start working for a competitor after working with you (some states may not allow this, so you need to check, or discuss with our experts)
  • Terms – it should also clearly state when payments will be made, how much they are and which currency they are in. You want no room for misunderstanding.
