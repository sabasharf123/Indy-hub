Filing Taxes


The golden rule to filing taxes is save money from each invoice to contribute to tax payments. If you don’t do this you can end up in trouble. Even if you’re tax payment isn’t due for 12 months just save a portion of each invoice you issue to pay towards it, otherwise you may find you need to pay your taxes and the money isn’t there. 

The next question is how much should you save, which is a little trickier to answer. The complexity of filing taxes will depend on your type of incorporation, number of employees, state of incorporation and the complexity in your business, but it can easily be 20-30% of your revenue.

There are several types of tax that you may need to pay including federal income tax, state business tax, social security, Medicare and personal income tax. As we said…it’s challenging. 

Fortunately, help is at hand, CreditKarma offer a free tax filing service for your personal tax, you may also want to thinking about hiring an accountant to assist with your bookkeeping and tax filing. Wave offers a great software package for invoice and time tracking – and for an additional fee they can provide bookkeeping or coaching on how to do it yourself. If you’d rather have a traditional accountant, PASBA is a great resource for finding them.

Filing taxes is one of the most challenging aspects of being a freelancer or small business, but you must make sure you comply with all tax policies otherwise you can face heavy fines and restrictions.
