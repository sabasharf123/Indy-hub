Getting Financing

There are numerous reasons you may want to get additional money into your company, it may be necessary to buy equipment, help you scale quicker or mean you don’t need to take the risk of investing your own money

Whatever the reason there are a few common sources of finance, each with very different pros and cons. We’ve outlined these below, and our experts are always available to assist in advising and helping you get investment. Before we get started there are a few terms we should clarify:

  • Debt – Debt is an amount of money given to you, which you promise to repay within a specified time period (normally 2-5 years) and with an interest rate. A bank loan is a form of debt. The interest rate is applied to the total remaining debt each year, and must also be paid. The interest is essentially the cost for taking the loan. 

  • Equity – this is a share in your company. An ‘equity holder’ will give you money to purchase this share. They may also have decision rights and be able to tell you to focus on specific strategy, or set of activities. They will also have a share of your profits, and if you sell the business, they will get a share of the money made. The most common source of equity are wealthy individuals with expertise in your sector.

    • Example: You are given $50,000 for a 25% equity stake. In the first year you make $40,00 profit, the equity holder will get 25% of this - $10,000 and you keep $30,000. In year two you sell the business for $200,000, the equity holder will get 25% of this $50,000 and you keep $150,000.

Personal investment or friends and family

Depending on your financial position and the investment needed, you may be able to put money into the business yourself. Given this is an exceptional situation we won’t dwell on it but a few pointers if you do decide to go down this path:

  • You may lose your money – no matter how great an idea you think you have it is always a risk and you may lose some or all of the money you invest

  • If you’re borrowing from friends or family be very clear if this is debt or equity and what terms are associated. Yes, it’s awkward to discuss this with friends and family, but it’s a lot more awkward to get two years down the line and realise they had very different expectations. We’d recommend putting a very simple contract in place, this can be a single A4 page, but something you can evidence what was agreed.

  • Be selective with who you borrow money from, note that it might change, and have an impact on, your relationships

Loans

Loans are a type of debt. Most banks offer small business loans and this is the most common form of financing for small business. Key points when considering a loan are:

  • Interest rate: as above, this impacts how much you’ll have to pay back. Depending on the bank, type of business and risk associated they will offer different rates. A typical interest rate is ~5-10% annual interest

  • Negotiate – bank loans are negotiable, although this is never advertised. A great tactic is if you have an offer from one bank, go to other banks and tell them that you’re deciding between the two. Also, ask if there is anything they can do to improve the deal, they may not change the interest rate but they may add additional sweeteners

  • Be careful who you approach – ‘loan sharks’ operate everywhere, including online, and may seem legitimate. They then use dishonest practices in order to get additional money from you. Make sure you check who you’re borrowing the money from before you do. Typical signs of loan sharks are high interest rates, poor reviews, hidden charges and unclear ownership structure

  • Collateral / security – most loans will require security. This is a physical item that can be reclaimed if you can’t repay the loan – such as a house. Some loans will be ‘unsecured’ but these providers will want to know your credit rating, and proof of earning.

 Angel or VC investors

Angel or VC investors are individuals or groups of people who are professional investors. They are interested in investing in high-growth start ups that they can make money from by making an equity investment. Angel and VC investing can be complex but a lot of advice is available, one of the best sources is Gust. A few points:

  • Make sure they’ve invested before. Many people now say they’re investors but haven’t invested before and can’t provide recommendations. A real investor won’t be offended if you ask for recommendations. 

  • They will likely take a large equity stake in your company. If you sell the company you could personally end up with significantly less than if you hadn’t brought on investment. You need to consider carefully how important investment is, and whether it’s make or break for your business

  • Investors have expertise. You should find investors who understand the sector you work in and can help you build your business. The advantage of an investors is not just their money, but their knowledge, experience, and contacts

  • An investor will want to see your business plan, understand the success you’ve had to date, why you’re excited about the idea and your experience and knowledge. They’re investing in you, your idea and your ability to deliver it. This can be a long process and involve a lot of meetings and preparation, but brining the right investor on can make your business grow exponentially

Crowdfunding

If you’re developing an on-trend consumer facing business crowd-funding could be an option. It’s also a great way of getting publicity. It typically involves promising certain things to those who contribute to your fund, such as free products or services once you start production. Crowd funding is still quite a difficult way of raising money but if you’re the next Sweetgreens, it’s worth a shot. The main crowd funding platforms are Kickstarter or Indiegogo

Micro-loan

Micro-loans have become increasingly popular way of raising money. As the name suggests they’re very similar to loans but for small amounts of ~$500-$5,000. There are numerous small loan organisations and most have a strong social mission. AEO is a great starting point.
