Hiring People

Hiring new people is an incredibly exiting part of a new business. You’ve proved the concept, you’re growing and now it’s time to put a team together! It can also be a risk, you’re paying this person from the profit of the company, and so you need to make sure that they’re adding value. To manage this risk here are our top tips:

  • Cut the dead weight– you can’t afford to have people not pulling their weight, so you have to be pretty brutal. It’s tough to let people go, but they’re not adding value, and they’re taking your time you’ve got to say goodbye

  • Use probation – every contract normally has a probation period of 1-6 months, however, it’s not normally taken that seriously. Use it seriously. After 1 month (or sooner!) if your new team member isn’t pulling their weight, you guessed it, cut them loose

  • Cultural fit – think about what sort of culture you want to create in the organisation and assess each interviewee against this. What sort of values and attitudes do you want them to have, and how can asses these? Ask them for real examples, then ask them for references, and make sure they actually played the role they say they did

  • Interviewing – interview is your moment to really understand someone. Make sure you’ve prepared and don’t just ask generic interview questions. Similar to culture questions, think about specifically what that person will do, and what you need them to prove. Also, think about what an exceptional person would do. Ask probing and detailed questions and keep asking how they specifically delivered it until you feel full comfortable you understand

  • Be patient – it’s hard, but be patient. You’ve just got a new contract and really need that person now, but if you wait then the rewards will be far bigger. If you rush the likelihood is you get someone who is ok, but not great, if you weight and line up the right person they can help your business grow
